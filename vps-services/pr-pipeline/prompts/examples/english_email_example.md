# Example: English-language pitch email

**Context:** Caroline Ravn is a Swedish sustainability entrepreneur pitching to The Local Sweden (English-language news site covering Sweden for expats and internationally-minded readers). She founded a circular fashion platform connecting Swedes with local clothing repair services. The "why now" hook is the EU's right-to-repair directive and rising repair service demand in Sweden.

---

**Subject:** Sweden's repair culture finds its app moment

**Body:**

Hi James,

Sweden has long had a reputation for environmental pragmatism, but something specific changed when the EU's right-to-repair directive took effect in March: searches for clothing repair services in Sweden jumped 34 percent in six weeks, according to Google Trends data for the Nordics.

Caroline Ravn saw this shift coming. In 2022 she launched Lapptacket, a platform that connects Swedish households with local tailors and repair workshops. It now has 80 partner craftspeople in 23 cities, and roughly half of its users had never paid for clothing repairs before signing up.

Your readers - many of whom moved to Sweden partly drawn by its sustainability credentials - might find it interesting to see this playing out in everyday life rather than in policy documents. Caroline can speak to what kinds of garments people are actually getting repaired, how the costs compare with buying new, and what local craftspeople are experiencing on the ground.

If this fits something you're planning for the spring, I'd be glad to set up a short call or send over some data from the platform.

Best,
[Sender name]

---

**Why this works:**
- Subject line frames the story as a cultural moment, not as a pitch.
- Opens directly with the news hook - a specific, timely data point.
- Client credentials are introduced briefly and factually, not boastfully.
- The "why it matters to your readers" paragraph directly addresses The Local's specific audience (expats interested in Swedish culture).
- The close is warm, specific, and offers two easy next steps.
- No bullet points. No em-dashes. No superlatives. Word count is approximately 210 words.
